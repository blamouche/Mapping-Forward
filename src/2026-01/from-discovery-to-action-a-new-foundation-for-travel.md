# From Discovery to Action: A New Foundation for Travel

**Source**: https://overturemaps.org/blog/2025/from-discovery-to-action-a-new-foundation-for-travel/

**Date**: December 1, 2025

**Author**: Albi Wiedersberg

**Keywords**: Overture Maps, Travel, Discovery, Places, Points of Interest, Travel Technology, Open Data

## Elevator pitch

The Overture Maps Foundation is building the data infrastructure that powers the entire travel journey, from initial discovery through booking and navigation to the final destination.

## Takeaways

- Overture Maps data supports the complete travel experience from discovery to action
- Open map data enables travel platforms to build more comprehensive and accurate services
- Places and points of interest data are critical components for travel applications
- The foundation's collaborative approach benefits the entire travel technology ecosystem
- Standardized, interoperable data reduces friction in travel planning and execution

## Synthesis

The Overture Maps Foundation is positioning itself as a foundational layer for the travel industry, providing the open map data that powers everything from initial destination discovery to final navigation and arrival. This comprehensive approach recognizes that modern travel experiences are built on reliable, consistent geographic information.

The travel industry has long struggled with fragmented data sources, where different platforms maintain their own databases of places, points of interest, and geographic features. This fragmentation leads to inconsistencies that frustrate travelers and limit the ability of travel platforms to deliver seamless experiences. The Overture Maps Foundation addresses this challenge by providing openly-licensed, standardized data that any platform can use.

The journey from discovery to action encompasses multiple stages where map data plays a critical role. During the discovery phase, travelers explore potential destinations, browse attractions, and research points of interest. Reliable place data ensures that travelers can find accurate information about restaurants, hotels, landmarks, and activities. Moving into the planning phase, routing and transportation data help travelers understand how to get between locations and estimate travel times.

The action phase—actually traveling—requires the most precise and up-to-date data. Navigation systems depend on accurate road networks, address databases, and real-time information to guide travelers safely to their destinations. Points of interest must be correctly located so that travelers can find the businesses and attractions they're seeking. The Overture Maps Foundation's emphasis on data quality and regular updates ensures that travel platforms can deliver reliable experiences.

For travel technology companies, building on open map data offers significant advantages. Rather than investing resources in maintaining proprietary geographic databases, companies can focus on building differentiated features and user experiences. The collaborative nature of the Overture Maps project means that improvements benefit the entire ecosystem, creating a virtuous cycle of data quality enhancement.

The foundation's approach also supports emerging travel use cases. As autonomous vehicles, delivery services, and new mobility options become more prevalent, the need for high-quality, standardized map data will only increase. By establishing open standards and collaborative data practices now, the Overture Maps Foundation is building infrastructure that will support the travel experiences of the future.

This vision of map data as foundational infrastructure for travel represents a shift in how the industry thinks about geographic information. Rather than treating map data as a competitive differentiator to be hoarded, the foundation's members recognize that shared, high-quality data raises the entire industry's capabilities and ultimately benefits travelers through better experiences across all platforms.
