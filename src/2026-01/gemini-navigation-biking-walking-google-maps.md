# Gemini in navigation is now available for walking and cycling in Google Maps

**Source**: https://blog.google/products-and-platforms/products/maps/gemini-navigation-biking-walking/

**Date**: January 29, 2026

**Author**: Google

**Keywords**: Google Maps, Gemini AI, navigation, walking, cycling, voice assistant, hands-free, mobile navigation, location intelligence

## Elevator pitch

Google expands Gemini AI integration in Maps to walking and cycling navigation, enabling pedestrians to use the AI as a personal tour guide and cyclists to stay hands-free while accessing information and sending messages.

## Takeaways

- Gemini AI is now available in Google Maps navigation for walking and biking, expanding beyond its initial driving-only functionality
- Pedestrians can use Gemini as a personal walking tour guide, asking questions about neighborhoods and getting dining recommendations along their route
- Cyclists can access hands-free assistance for safety, including ETA checks, calendar events, and sending messages while maintaining handlebar control
- The AI leverages Maps' current location data to provide contextually relevant suggestions aligned with the user's route
- The feature is rolling out globally on both iOS and Android platforms wherever Gemini is accessible

## Synthesis

Google has announced a significant expansion of its Gemini AI integration within Google Maps, bringing intelligent navigation assistance to pedestrians and cyclists. Previously available only for driving navigation, this update makes Gemini's conversational AI capabilities accessible across all major transportation modes supported by the mapping platform.

For pedestrians, the integration transforms the navigation experience by positioning Gemini as what Google describes as a "personal walking tour guide." Users can engage with the AI through voice commands to explore their surroundings while walking. The assistant responds to queries about neighborhood identification, helping users understand the character and context of areas they're traversing. When users seek dining recommendations, Gemini draws from Google Maps' comprehensive location data to suggest restaurants and cafes that align with the user's current route, providing practical suggestions that don't require significant detours.

The cycling implementation prioritizes safety above all else, addressing the fundamental challenge that cyclists face when trying to access information on their phones while riding. By enabling hands-free operation through voice commands, Gemini allows riders to maintain proper handlebar control at all times. Cyclists can request estimated arrival times to track their progress, check upcoming calendar events to stay on top of their schedules, or send text messages to contacts without removing their hands from the handlebars. The example Google provides is telling a contact through voice command that "I'm 10 minutes behind" - a common real-world scenario for cyclists running late to appointments.

The underlying technology leverages the real-time location awareness that Google Maps already possesses. Rather than providing generic AI responses, Gemini can contextualize its answers and suggestions based on the user's current position and intended route. This location-aware intelligence enables more relevant and immediately actionable assistance compared to general-purpose AI assistants.

The feature is rolling out globally on both iOS and Android platforms, though availability depends on Gemini's regional accessibility. This global deployment reflects Google's strategy of making advanced AI capabilities available across its core products rather than limiting them to specific markets or platforms.

The expansion represents Google's continued investment in making navigation more intelligent and conversational, moving beyond turn-by-turn directions toward a more interactive and assistive experience for all modes of transportation.
