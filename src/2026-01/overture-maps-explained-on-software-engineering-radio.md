# Overture Maps Explained on Software Engineering Radio

**Source**: https://overturemaps.org/blog/2025/overture-maps-explained-on-software-engineering-radio/

**Date**: December 11, 2025

**Author**: Overture Maps Foundation

**Keywords**: Overture Maps, Software Engineering Radio, Podcast, Open Map Data, Linux Foundation, Geospatial

## Elevator pitch

The Overture Maps Foundation featured on the Software Engineering Radio podcast to explain their open map data initiative to the broader software engineering community.

## Takeaways

- Overture Maps Foundation appeared on Software Engineering Radio, a well-known podcast for software engineers
- The episode provides an accessible introduction to the Overture Maps project for developers
- Overture Maps operates under the Linux Foundation as an open map data initiative
- The podcast format helps reach software engineers who may not be familiar with geospatial technologies
- Media outreach demonstrates the foundation's commitment to community engagement and education

## Synthesis

The Overture Maps Foundation has expanded its outreach efforts by featuring on Software Engineering Radio, one of the most respected podcasts in the software development community. This appearance represents an important step in bringing awareness of open mapping technologies to a broader audience of software engineers and developers.

Software Engineering Radio has long been a trusted resource for software professionals seeking in-depth technical discussions. By appearing on this platform, the Overture Maps Foundation gains access to an audience that may not traditionally follow geospatial technology developments but could benefit significantly from understanding open map data availability and usage.

The Overture Maps Foundation, operating under the Linux Foundation umbrella, has been working to create high-quality, openly-licensed map data through collaboration between major technology companies including Amazon, Meta, Microsoft, and TomTom. The podcast appearance provides an opportunity to explain the foundation's mission, technical architecture, and the benefits of open map data to developers who might integrate such data into their applications.

For many software engineers, mapping functionality is something they interact with through APIs provided by major platforms. The Overture Maps initiative offers an alternative approach where the underlying data itself is open and accessible, enabling new use cases and reducing dependency on proprietary mapping services. Explaining these concepts through a conversational podcast format makes the information more accessible than technical documentation alone.

The appearance on Software Engineering Radio also demonstrates the foundation's maturation as an organization. Effective communication and community building are essential for open-source projects to thrive, and targeted media outreach helps establish credibility and attract contributors. Developers who learn about Overture Maps through trusted channels like this podcast may be more likely to explore the project and potentially contribute to its development.

This type of developer education and outreach is crucial for the long-term success of open mapping initiatives. As more developers understand the availability and potential of open map data, the ecosystem around these resources will grow, creating new tools, applications, and integrations that benefit the broader community. The Software Engineering Radio appearance is part of a larger effort to build awareness and adoption of open geospatial standards and data resources.
