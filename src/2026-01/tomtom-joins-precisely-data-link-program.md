# TomTom Joins Precisely Data Link Program

**Source**: https://www.precisely.com/press-release/tomtom-joins-precisely-data-link-program-bringing-authoritative-map-and-street-data-to-the-pre%E2%80%91linked-data-ecosystem/

**Date**: January 28, 2026

**Author**: Precisely

**Keywords**: TomTom, Precisely, Data Link, data integration, map data, location intelligence, geospatial data, partnerships, interoperability

## Elevator pitch

TomTom has joined Precisely's Data Link program, deepening a 20-year partnership and enabling organizations to more easily integrate authoritative map and street data with other trusted datasets for improved decision-making.

## Takeaways

- Data Link is a partner program that provides pre-linked datasets across providers, eliminating the complexity of connecting multi-source data
- The partnership deepens a 20-year relationship between Precisely and TomTom in delivering location data solutions
- TomTom joins industry leaders like Dun & Bradstreet, Overture Maps Foundation, GeoX Analytics, and Regrid in the Data Link ecosystem
- Use cases include last-mile delivery optimization, risk assessment, urban planning, site selection, and supply chain management
- The integration enables organizations to enhance transportation efficiency, traffic management, and location-based decision making

## Synthesis

Precisely announced that TomTom, the Dutch specialist in mapping and location technology, has joined its Data Link program in a move that deepens an already substantial partnership spanning two decades. The announcement represents a significant development in the geospatial data integration landscape, making it easier for organizations to combine authoritative map data with complementary datasets.

Data Link is Precisely's partner program designed to address one of the most persistent challenges in enterprise data management: the complexity of integrating datasets from multiple providers. Rather than requiring organizations to undertake time-consuming and resource-intensive data linking efforts, Data Link provides an ecosystem of datasets that are pre-linked across participating vendors. This approach dramatically reduces the technical overhead and costs associated with multi-source data integration.

The relationship between Precisely and TomTom is not new. Precisely has relied on TomTom to deliver the location data powering many of its solutions for over 20 years. This formal inclusion in the Data Link program, however, represents an evolution of the partnership that provides customers with more seamless access to TomTom's comprehensive map and street data offerings.

TomTom's contribution to the Data Link ecosystem centers on its industry-leading map data solutions. These datasets enable organizations to enhance transportation efficiency, optimize traffic management and routing decisions, and make more informed choices regarding location planning and urban development. The practical applications span multiple industries and use cases, including last-mile delivery accuracy for logistics providers, risk assessment and underwriting for insurance companies, urban planning for municipalities, site selection for retailers and businesses, and supply chain management for enterprises.

The expansion of Data Link with TomTom's addition follows the program's growth to include other prominent data providers. Industry leaders already participating in the ecosystem include Dun & Bradstreet, the Overture Maps Foundation, GeoX Analytics, and Regrid. This constellation of data providers creates a comprehensive ecosystem where organizations can access trusted, high-quality data from multiple authoritative sources without the integration burden that traditionally accompanies multi-vendor data strategies.

The announcement reflects a broader industry trend toward data ecosystems and interoperability, recognizing that effective location intelligence increasingly requires the combination of multiple specialized datasets rather than reliance on any single provider.
